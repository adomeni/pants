Step 2: Laying/Cutting Out Pieces

We already learned about the straight grain of the pattern pieces so let's learn about the grain of your fabric. For the best results, the legs of your pants must be cut following both the straight grain of the pattern and the fabric.

Grain of Fabric

So how do you know if you are cutting your pieces on the straight grain of the fabric? It’s quite simple! Fold your fabric width-wise in half so that the “raw edges” are lining up perfectly. Iron the folded edge flat and make sure that the fabric is laying flat on the ironing board. Next, hold up the fabric at each end and look for any pulling or drooping of the fabric. If the fabric falls in the same direction all throughout, you’re good to go. You may need to re-iron the fabric in event of the fabric not sitting properly.

Pinning Down Pieces

Ok, deep breath. You can finally start pinning your pieces down. Start with bigger pieces like the front and back legs and arrange everything around those. Make sure the straight grain of the pattern pieces on the legs match the straight grain of the fabric (align with the raw edges). You only need to use 1 front and 1 back piece since you are cutting them from folded fabric.


NOTE: some pattern pieces might need to be placed on the fold of the fabric. For example the waistband, fly guard, etc.


OTHER NOTE: You will need to place the riser on top of the back pants piece and overlap them to form one single piece that is the same length as the front leg piece.
