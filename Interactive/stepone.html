Step 1: Making the Pants Pattern

Following a store bought pattern would be useless for this tutorial because they always come with instructions. I would like to teach you a very simple way to make a pattern that works very well! All you need is an old pair of your favourite pants and a seam ripper!

Begin by taking the pants apart using the seam ripper. Make sure you save every piece that you detach even if you don’t think you’ll need it. Once everything is apart you should roughly have the following pieces:

  • 2 front legs
  • 2 back legs
  • Front pocket pieces
  • Zipper
  • Fly facing
  • Fly guard
  • Waistband
  • Back pockets
  • Riser pieces

These will be our pattern pieces! You can now go ahead and trace these onto thin paper if you’d like or we can just use the pieces the way they are. Just a few more steps before we begin sewing.

"Straight Grain"

Here's a quick tip:

It is super important to know what the straight grain of your fabric is. This term refers to the direction that the threads are arranged in the fabric. Pant legs fall straight because they are cut on the straight grain of the fabric and if you were to cut them off grain, they would turn inward or outward. Crooked pant legs are super annoying so try your best to be accurate. To find the grain of the front and back pant legs, pull a thread from the bottom of each leg all the way up to the top. The thread should pull out in a vertical line. This will be very helpful later on.

"Notches/Markings"

Make your pant legs align perfectly by following these steps:

  • Pin the front pants to the back following the straight grain
  • Look to see if the sides of the pants follow a similar curve (match them if not)
  • Align the sides together and make triangle notches near the hip, knee and bottom leg
