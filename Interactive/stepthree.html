Step 3: Front Pants

The main features of the front pants are the zipper and the pockets. The zipper is a bit tricky to understand so just follow my fool proof method and everything will work out fine! I promise.

Important Pieces

  • Zipper
  • Fly Facing
  • Fly Guard

Zipper

Put simply

  • Match the fronts with faces together and stitch from the bottom of the crotch curve up to about a quarter inch away from the length of the fly guard
  • Take the right side pant and cut a slit at the length of the fly guard about 1/4 inch in; fold this seam and press it with an iron
  • Place the edge of the folded seam on top of the zipper, with the fly guard underneath and line the edge up with the left edge of the zipper
  • Sew the zipper and fly guard to the front pants all the way down to the bottom of the fly guard
  • Sew the fly facing to the front of the left side pant, fold it under and press the seam
  • Sew down the edge of the left pant crotch, to the bottom of the fly facing
  • Pin the front pants closed, flip them over and pin the other side of the zipper to the fly facing
  • Sew the zipper to the fly facing
  • Starting at the bottom of the fly facing, follow the outer edge and stitch the facing to the front pants
  • Sew the rest of the front seam closed from the initial stitch, to the bottom of the zipper

pockets

For the pockets, you will need to use the 2 pocket lining pieces that were cut out of the fabric. If you cut them on the fold, you will still need to cut the pocket shape out of one side of each piece. Once this is complete, line the curve of the lining pieces with the curve of front pockets and stitch them together. You will now need to fold the lining pieces to the back of the pants pieces, iron them flat, and top stitch the edge of the pants pocket. This may be confusing but if you really sit down and think about it, it gets easy!

At this point, the lining should be attached to the front pants at the pocket curves. You may need to iron the pocket lining fold again so it sits properly.
